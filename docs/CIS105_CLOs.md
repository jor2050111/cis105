## Official Course Description

Overview of computer technology, concepts, terminology, and the role of
computers in business and society. Discussion of social and ethical
issues related to computers. Use of word processing, spreadsheet,
database, and presentation software. Includes uses of application
software and the Internet for efficient and effective problem solving.
Exploration of relevant emerging technologies.

**Requisites:** None.

## Elevated Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

Each CLO carries its Revised Bloom's Taxonomy level. MLOs in every
chapter must ladder up to these. The elevated wording raises the
district verb only where the assessment genuinely reaches that level.

* **CLO I (Analyze):** Differentiate the hardware components of a
  computer system and explain how each supports processing, input,
  output, and storage
* **CLO II (Analyze):** Classify system and application software and
  match each type to the tasks it serves
* **CLO III (Understand):** Explain how networks share resources and
  support communication in homes and businesses
* **CLO IV (Understand):** Describe the technologies behind websites,
  including browsers, HTML, and web servers
* **CLO V (Apply):** Use the Internet to communicate, collaborate, and
  retrieve credible information
* **CLO VI (Analyze):** Examine system security and privacy risks and
  the safeguards that reduce them
* **CLO VII (Create):** Create macros and explain the basics of Visual
  Basic for Applications (VBA)
* **CLO VIII (Understand):** Outline the steps for planning and
  implementing a technology solution, from needs analysis to rollout
* **CLO IX (Evaluate):** Evaluate tasks and problems to select the
  technology tools and resources that fit
* **CLO X (Understand):** Explain the terminology, uses, and limits of
  technology in business and society
* **CLO XI (Evaluate):** Judge technology use against ethical and
  social standards, including the consequences of misuse
* **CLO XII (Understand):** Describe how the departments of a business
  and a range of career paths put technology to work
* **CLO XIII (Apply):** Produce documents, spreadsheets, databases, and
  presentations with industry-standard productivity software

## District CLOs (Verbatim)

The official district wording, for alignment audits. The parenthetical
numerals cite the district outline sections each CLO covers.

* I. Identify common hardware components of computer systems and
  describe their uses. (I)
* II. Identify different types of software and their uses. (II)
* III. Describe common uses of networks. (III)
* IV. Describe Website Technology. (IV)
* V. Use the Internet to communicate, collaborate, and retrieve
  information. (IV, VII)
* VI. Explore system security and privacy issues. (III, IV, VII)
* VII. Create macros and understand the basics of Visual Basic for
  Applications (VBA). (V)
* VIII. Describe the steps in planning and implementing technology
  solutions. (VI)
* IX. Determine when technology is useful and select the appropriate
  tool(s) and technology resources to address a variety of tasks and
  problems. (VI, VIII)
* X. Identify terminology and the uses of technology in business and
  society, including limitations. (VI)
* XI. Identify positive social and ethical behaviors when using
  technology and the consequences of misuse. (VII)
* XII. Describe how technology is used in the departments of a business
  and in various career paths. (VIII)
* XIII. Use word processing, spreadsheet, database, and presentation
  software. (IX)

thread: Word in Chapters 1-3, PowerPoint in Chapters 4-5, Excel in
Chapters 6-9, Airtable in Chapters 10-11, and Excel macros with VBA in

docs/part-structure.md.

Mr. Vega's standing course decision replaces Access with Airtable (the
substitution is recorded twice in the source outline). Chapters teach
the same database concepts (tables, fields, records, queries, reports)
in Airtable.

   D. Output Devices

      1. Word Processing
      2. Spreadsheets
      3. Databases
      4. Presentation Software

   A. Types of Networks
      1. Local Area Network (LAN)
      2. Wide Area Network (WAN)
   B. Uses of Networks
      1. Sharing Resources
      2. Communication
   C. Security and Data Integrity

IV. Internet
   A. History
   B. World Wide Web Structure
   C. Browsers
   D. Search Engines
   E. E-mail
   F. e-Commerce
   G. Hypertext Markup Language (HTML)
   H. Security
   I. Intranet
   J. Websites

      1. Procedures
      2. Variables
   B. Macros
      1. Excel
      2. Access

VI. Planning and Implementing Technology Solutions

            1. Pivot tables

         a. Design data
         b. Create tables
      2. Manage data
         a. Filter
         b. Sort
         c. Query
         d. Report
         e. Pivot table
            1. View
            2. Chart
         f. Structured Query Language (SQL)
